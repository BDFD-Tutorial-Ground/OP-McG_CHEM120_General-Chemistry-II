Problem Based Learning (PBL)

General Information and Instructions

"Problem-based learning (PBL) is a pedagogy that uses real-world problems to direct students towards the learning objectives of a course. Solving the problems requires that students build upon their background knowledge and gain new subject knowledge of the material that they have not yet studied. They gain this knowledge on a need to know basis in order to solve the problem." (PBL Defined | Problem-Based Learning: Chemistry )

The CHEM-120 PBL lab consists of two parts, PBL 1 and PBL 2. Here is a general outline of the PBL process:

  1. During the Kinetics experiment, you will find a partner to work with and choose a problem to work on from the four problems provided (see the PBL 1 Problem List tab under "Content/Problem Based Learning")
  2. You will then read additional information provided for your problem BEFORE coming to the PBL 1 lab (Feb 10-14, 2025 for BOTH groups A and B)
  3. During the PBL 1 you will work with you partner under your TA's guidance to propose an experimental solution to the problem
  4. After the PBL 1 lab, you will submit your proposal on MyCourses under the PBL 1 Assignment
  5. Your TA will evaluate and approve your proposal as well suggest changes if needed
  6. During the PBL 2 (Mar 24 - Apr 4, 2025) you will perform the experiment you proposed
  7. After the PBL 2 lab you will write and submit your report on your experiment using the Lab Report Template provided for each problem

Part 1: PBL 1 (PLEASE BRING YOUR ELECTRONIC DEVICE)

Before the lab:

  • Choose from one of the four problems provided to you
  • Perform a quick Internet search to start looking for possible solutions
  • Read the additional information provided for your experiment BEFORE coming to the lab

During the lab:

  • In the lab, fill the table "What do we know/What we need to know"
  • Search for the information you need to know. 
  • Your TA will guide you through the process and provide extra material/information if needed
  • Propose an experimental solution to your problem 
  • Find a procedure
  • Write down the list of chemicals and apparatus you will need for your experiment

After the lab:

  • Prepare the report using the lab Report Template provided.
  • YOU HAVE 7 DAYS TO SUBMIT YOUR REPORT
  • EACH STUDENT SUBMITS AN INDIVIDUAL COPY OF THE REPORT
  • The report should include
    • A brief outline of the proposed solution
    • A list of chemicals and apparatus including the quantities
    • An experimental procedure
    • References
  • The PBL 1 Lab Report is worth 30 pts

Part II: PBL 2

In the second part of the Problem Based Learning (PBL2) you will be performing experiments to test whether your proposed solution works.

These are the steps you need to do.

Before the lab:

  • Read the feedback your TA has provided for your PBL1 report
  • Modify your procedure accordingly (where applicable). Make sure you are working on a SMALL SCALE
  • Obtain the necessary materials such as pencils, batteries, oil, etc. that you are planning to bring to the lab (where applicable)
  • Print the procedure you are going to use in the lab and bring it with you to the lab
  • Prepare your own Data Sheet template. Make sure to include an observation section. 
  • Print your personalized PBL2 Data Sheet and bring it with you to the lab
If you do not have your printed finalized procedure with you when you come to the lab, you will be asked to reschedule your lab and a penalty will apply.

During the lab:

  • Show your printed final procedure to your TA and get it signed
  • Collect all the materials you need based on your procedure
  • Perform your experiment and record all your observations on the Data Sheet 
  • Get your Data Sheet signed by your TA before leaving the lab
  • Dispose of all the waste into a designated container
  • Clean up all your glassware and return it to the place where you took it from

After the lab:

  • Prepare the report USING THE LAB REPORT TEMPLATE provided.
  • Please submit your report in 2 days from your lab together with your signed procedure and Data Sheet as a single pdf file
  • EACH STUDENT SUBMITS AN INDIVIDUAL COPY OF THE REPORT
  • The PBL 1 Lab Report is worth 30 pts
